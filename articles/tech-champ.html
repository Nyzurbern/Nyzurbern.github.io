My Journey as a Tech Champ

2024: Becoming a PLD Ambassador

In Sec 2, after a landslide vote in my class, I was elected as the PLD Ambassador (the previous name for Tech Champ) in my class for the year. Historically, I have always been very good at solving tech-related issues for teachers and my classmates even in primary school, so this role came naturally to me. As a tech champ, my main responsibility was just setting up the projector screen for teachers before class and ensuring all tech equipment worked by reporting any faults to the school's ICT department.

We had some basic training on how to do the responsibilities of a PLD Ambassador, but otherwise, the year was pretty uneventful. Towards the end of the year though, us PLD Ambassadors were given the opportunity to participate in the Tech Champ Camp. It was an annual event hosted by 4 iPad schools incluidng ours, where sec 1/2 tech champs from various iPad schools gathered and us host schools of sec 2/3 tech champs would organise a workshop. This year we organised a workshop on google's AI Teachable Machine.

As a presenter, I had the opportunity to walk through the steps of using the Google Teachable Machine and demonstrate its capabilities to the other tech champs, so that they could use scratch to make a solution to a real-world problem, like say, monitoring utility consumption. We then graded their presentations based on a rubric and the winner was in the final round. Overall, this taught me a lot about how to present in a workshop and how to mentor juniors from different schools.

2025: Re-electing as Tech Champ

In Sec 3, I was re-elected as the Tech Champ for my class since the role of tech champ is not continuous year after year like say student councilors. This year, I had more responsibilities than before, including helping teachers with tech-related issues and assisting in the school's events. One week, we had to host a mental well-being booth where students would do a kahoot quiz on supportive friendships and win prizes for doing well on it.

We also engaged in VIA activities this year, including making an app on Figma for SG:60 aimed at making inclusivity and empathy a reality for those with special needs. The project was titled "Walk In My Shoes: Designing for Empathy." It was showcased in a booth-style event hosted in our school with many other schools coming and showing off their own apps made on figma for SG:60. We went on to win the people's choice award!

Another highlight was this year's tech champ. The format was similar but this time we had to teach about the robotic platform called Stick 'Em. As the name suggests, they are robots built mostly out of wooden chopsticks and be programmed and remote-controlled to move around and complete tasks.

I was not a presenter this time, but I still had the opportunity to assist the groups of tech champs with their projects.
