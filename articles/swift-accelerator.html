Swift Accelerator

Overview

In 2025, I participated in the Swift Accelerator program, an 8-month endeavor where I learnt to build iOS apps using Swift and SwiftUI. Over the course of the program, I developed 3 apps, each with its own unique features and functionalities, with the last one launched on the app store. The program takes a challenged based approach for making apps with each challenge progressively getting more difficult and requiring a deeper understanding of Swift and SwiftUI.

Challenges

Collaborative

Working with a team of students from different schools was a challenge. We had to learn to communicate effectively and work together to achieve our goals. There were also many arguments on app design and functionality as well as on scrutinising those who weren't doing as much work. I remember in challenge 3 where one of my groupmates missed over 2 weeks of sessions and when he came in, he suddenly started arguing with us over our app's idea. It was difficult getting over hurdles like this but with the support of our amazing mentors and instructors along with being patient with each other, we were able to overcome these challenges.

Technical

Learning to work with SwiftUI and understanding its declarative approach to UI development, was challenging. It was quite different what I had faced before as I had never written my UI in code before. I also had to learn to work with SwiftData, which was a new framework for me, and it was my first time learning how data persistence works on a client level. Moreover, the way iOS app development works meant that swift was used in conjunction with swiftui in the same file which took some time getting used to.

What I learned

I learnt a lot in SAP. I learnt how to ideate, design & build apps from scratch and how to work in a team and communicate effectively. I also got the opportunity to attain 2 certifications, those being App Development with Swift Associate and App Development with Swift Certified User. Overall, the Swift Accelerator program was a great learning experience and I am grateful for the opportunity to have participated in it.
